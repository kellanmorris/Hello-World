<center> <b> Transcript From Knox's Three Tracts </b>
If the purchasing a Negroe for a slave be an infringement of devine and human laws,
in God's name, why is such a trade permitted? A few words in act of parliament
prohibiting the importation or sale of Negroes in our colonies will destroy the
practive in future. --- And a few words more, declaring the offspring of Negroes
already imported to be free, will prevent slavery extending to the next generation.
Annotations:
If purchasing a Slave is an "infriengement of devine and human laws"
then WHY is it being allowed? The act of importing or selling slaves in our colony is prohibited,
and will hurt our future if we continue.

.... Declaring the offspring of slaves to already be imported to be free,
will further prevent slavery continuing in the future generations.
